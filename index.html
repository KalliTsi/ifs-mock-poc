// Company profile
ISS UK & Ireland — Integrated Facilities Management at enterprise scale
ISS is one of the world's largest FM providers, operating across government, healthcare, transport, commercial real estate, education, and retail in the UK and Ireland. Services span cleaning, catering, technical engineering (M&E), planned and reactive building maintenance, security, and grounds management. Tens of thousands of field-based placemakers deployed daily across hundreds of client sites via a hybrid model of directly employed engineers and a managed subcontractor network.

Major contracts include the DWP (£135M, 7-year IFM), Defra, the FCDO (£34M, 5-year), Northern Trains (477 sites), Virgin Media O2, Unibail-Rodamco-Westfield, and Warwick Hospital.

// Use case & problem
Multi-site IFM delivery across complex, regulated estates
ISS manages thousands of assets and work orders across hundreds of client sites simultaneously — a combination of planned preventive maintenance (PPM), reactive fault response, and compliance-driven inspections covering M&E, fire safety, lifts, and building fabric. The operational challenge is structurally complex: a hybrid workforce of directly employed engineers and subcontractors, strict SLA commitments baked into government contracts, and a mandatory requirement to evidence compliance across regulated estates including healthcare and central government.
Operational friction
Disconnected scheduling, parts inventory, and subcontractor visibility create repeat call-outs and SLA exposure. Engineer performance varies significantly across a large, distributed workforce of mixed seniority — fault diagnosis relies on individual knowledge rather than a structured, repeatable process.

Compliance & knowledge risk
Regulated estates require auditable evidence of every maintenance action. As ISS mobilises large new contracts (DWP, Defra) and rapidly onboards new workforces, standardising engineer quality and ensuring consistent compliance documentation becomes operationally critical at scale.

// 03 — Identified Pain Points
Stage 01 · Detect
Reactive, not predictive
Faults are discovered by occupants or on scheduled visits, not by sensors. BMS data exists but isn't wired to work order triggers — the asset is already failing before anyone knows.
Stage 02 · Log & Triage
No single source of truth
Multiple intake channels (phone, email, portal, direct to engineer) create duplicates and inconsistent records. Asset service history is not automatically surfaced at intake, so priority is set blind.
Stage 03 · Plan & Resource
Parts and skills mismatch
Parts availability is unknown at planning time. The wrong grade of engineer is often dispatched — a costly mistake that requires a second visit and burns SLA time before work even begins.
Stage 04 · Dispatch
Subcontractor visibility gap
When subcontractors are used, real-time location, availability, and skills data is missing from the planner view. Briefing is ad hoc — engineers arrive without a pre-diagnosis or contextual job pack.
Stages 05–06 · Arrive & Diagnose
Diagnosis depends on who shows up
Fault diagnosis relies entirely on individual expertise. A junior engineer on the same asset as a senior takes significantly longer, is more likely to miss the root cause, and less likely to fix first time.
Stage 08 · Document
Compliance is manual and inconsistent
On regulated estates (NHS, government) every maintenance action must be evidenced. Documentation is captured by the engineer manually, in inconsistent formats, creating high audit preparation overhead.
Stage 09 · Close & Learn
No feedback loop to planning
SLA breaches are discovered retrospectively. Root cause data captured in the field is not fed back into asset maintenance schedules or used to improve future dispatch decisions.
// 04 — How Might We · Opportunity Questions
Stages 05–06 · Diagnose
How might we give any engineer the diagnostic capability of the best engineer on that asset — regardless of their individual experience?
Stages 05–06 · Context
How might we surface schematics, service history, and live BMS readings as a single briefing — before the engineer physically intervenes?
Stages 01–02 · Detect
How might we predict failures before they impact occupants by connecting BMS sensor data to work order triggers automatically?
Stage 03 · Plan
How might we eliminate aborted visits by confirming parts availability and engineer skill match before the job is dispatched?
Stage 08 · Document
How might we auto-generate audit-ready compliance evidence at the point of work — with zero manual input from the engineer?
Stage 04 · Dispatch
How might we give planners real-time visibility of all engineers — directly employed and subcontracted — in a single dispatch view?
// 05 — Where Resolve Adds Value
Resolve is the point-of-work intelligence layer — it activates at stages 5–8, bridging EAM asset data and FSM job management at the exact moment the engineer is on-site. It doesn't replace either system; it makes both more effective by eliminating the knowledge gap between the office and the field.
Stage 05 · On-Site Arrival
Rich job briefing
Schematics, O&M manuals, full service history, and live BMS readings surfaced in-context on the engineer's mobile — before they open the panel.
Stage 06 · Diagnose
AI fault diagnosis
Engineer captures fault via video, image, or audio. Resolve delivers AI-assisted root cause identification — standardising diagnosis quality across the full workforce.
Stage 07 · Repair
Guided repair workflow
Step-by-step SOP guidance, torque specs, and safety checklists surfaced in the workflow — reducing variation between engineers and improving first-time fix rate.
Stage 08 · Document
Compliance auto-generated
Evidence captured through Resolve (video, image, voice notes) is automatically structured into an audit-ready compliance record — zero manual form-filling.
// 06 — Assumptions to Test in Discovery
A-01Engineers currently have no structured access to schematics or manuals while on-site — diagnosis is done from memory or by calling a colleague.
A-02First-time fix rates vary significantly by engineer seniority — junior engineers on complex M&E assets have materially lower FTFR than senior counterparts.
A-03Compliance documentation on regulated estates (DWP, NHS) is currently captured manually and requires significant rework before audit submission.
A-04BMS data is available and structured enough to trigger predictive maintenance work orders — but the integration to FSM/EAM work order creation has not been built.
A-05The gap in diagnostic quality between direct employees and subcontractors is a known planner concern — particularly on newly mobilised contracts.
A-06SLA penalty clauses in government contracts make even a 5–10% improvement in first-time fix rate financially material at the contract level.
