// Company profile
ISS UK & Ireland — Integrated Facilities Management at enterprise scale
ISS is one of the world's largest FM providers, operating across government, healthcare, transport, commercial real estate, education, and retail in the UK and Ireland. Services span cleaning, catering, technical engineering (M&E), planned and reactive building maintenance, security, and grounds management. Tens of thousands of field-based placemakers deployed daily across hundreds of client sites via a hybrid model of directly employed engineers and a managed subcontractor network.

Major contracts include the DWP (£135M, 7-year IFM), Defra, the FCDO (£34M, 5-year), Northern Trains (477 sites), Virgin Media O2, Unibail-Rodamco-Westfield, and Warwick Hospital.

// Use case & problem
Multi-site IFM delivery across complex, regulated estates
ISS manages thousands of assets and work orders across hundreds of client sites simultaneously — a combination of planned preventive maintenance (PPM), reactive fault response, and compliance-driven inspections covering M&E, fire safety, lifts, and building fabric. The operational challenge is structurally complex: a hybrid workforce of directly employed engineers and subcontractors, strict SLA commitments baked into government contracts, and a mandatory requirement to evidence compliance across regulated estates including healthcare and central government.
Operational friction
Disconnected scheduling, parts inventory, and subcontractor visibility create repeat call-outs and SLA exposure. Engineer performance varies significantly across a large, distributed workforce of mixed seniority — fault diagnosis relies on individual knowledge rather than a structured, repeatable process.
SLA penalty risk Repeat call-outs Parts availability
Compliance & knowledge risk
Regulated estates require auditable evidence of every maintenance action. As ISS mobilises large new contracts (DWP, Defra) and rapidly onboards new workforces, standardising engineer quality and ensuring consistent compliance documentation becomes operationally critical at scale.

// Value proposition
Operations layer — connected field management
A unified platform connecting job scheduling, M&E parts inventory, subcontractor dispatch, compliance record-keeping, and audit trail generation across all client sites. Planners gain real-time visibility across the entire hybrid workforce — directly employed and subcontracted — eliminating the data fragmentation that drives SLA breaches and reactive inefficiencies.
Scheduling & dispatch Subcontractor management Parts inventory integration Compliance documentation
Field layer — Resolve AI fault diagnosis
Engineers use Resolve to capture faults via video and audio, receive AI-assisted diagnosis, and follow guided repair workflows — reducing reliance on individual expertise and standardising quality across a large, geographically dispersed team. Resolve integrates a rich set of contextual data to guide engineers at the exact point of work:
Knowledge integration
ESM service history, equipment manuals, standard operating procedures (SOPs), and O&M documentation surfaced in-context — so engineers have the right information at the moment of fault, not back at the depot.
Technical data integration
Building schematics, asset configuration data, and live sensor and BMS readings fed into the diagnostic workflow — enabling AI-assisted root cause identification before the engineer physically intervenes.

Contract-level financial case
In IFM contracts, SLA failure triggers defined financial penalties — making the ROI case more precise than in typical service contexts. Every improvement in first-time fix rate has a directly quantifiable downside avoided. With ISS managing contracts worth hundreds of millions across multi-year terms, even marginal SLA improvements carry material financial impact.
Workforce scale & onboarding
As ISS rapidly mobilises large new contract workforces — DWP (2025), Defra — Resolve accelerates onboarding by embedding knowledge into the engineer's workflow. The gap between a senior M&E specialist and a less experienced operative narrows when both are guided by the same AI-assisted diagnostic process, SOPs, schematics, and live sensor data.
